vis (audio visualiser)

Easy Python-based FFmpeg wrapper for audio visualisation

vis is a visualiser created to allow users to upload audio files as videos where embedded audio is not normally supported.
As an example, despite Discord featuring support for embedded audio files on their PC and web platforms for years, it seems they are apathetic about adding mobile support for it, which means most audio can't be played on mobile without downloading the file.
Using vis means it plays as a video file instead, which Discord natively supports on all platforms.

A basic visualiser would have worked fine, but had a couple of limitations. Videos of the wrong aspect ratios would suffer when online video players would overlay their video play bar on top; it could also become complicated to use some vertical volume bars. Various visualisation methods would also encode into a format that didn't work on specific devices; iPhones use a different format encoding of h264 to Android and PC.

Features

  • Generates video files designed to be shared in online settings, for example Discord or Twitter/X, which don't support audio embeds in all situations
  • Timecode field is raised slightly to allow for the play bar that most online video players use
  • Supports custom author/title fields, plus custom output destination
  • Dynamic moving background
  • Available from the CLI with parameters; similar to FFmpeg itself but makes it easier to work with its advanced options and parameters.
